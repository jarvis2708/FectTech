What is SEO?

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. It's the practice of optimizing your website to increase its visibility in organic (non-paid) search engine results.

When someone searches for information, products, or services on Google, Bing, or other search engines, they typically click on one of the top results. SEO helps your website rank higher in these results, driving more traffic to your site.

üí° Key Point

SEO is about understanding what people are searching for online, the answers they're seeking, and the words they're using. Then, optimizing your content to match these search queries.

How Search Engines Work

Understanding how search engines work is fundamental to SEO. Search engines use three main processes:

1

Crawling

Search engines use automated bots (called crawlers or spiders) to discover and scan websites across the internet.

2

Indexing

After crawling, search engines store and organize the information they find in a massive database called an index.

3

Ranking

When users search, search engines analyze the index to provide the most relevant results, ranked by hundreds of factors.

Key SEO Concepts

Familiarize yourself with these fundamental SEO terms:

Keywords
Words and phrases users type into search engines. Targeting the right keywords is crucial for SEO success.
Meta Tags
HTML elements that describe page content. Includes title tags and meta descriptions.
Backlinks
Links from other websites to yours. Search engines view these as "votes of confidence."
Domain Authority
A score predicting how well a website will rank. Higher authority means better ranking potential.
Organic Traffic
Visitors who find your site through search engine results, not paid advertisements.
SERP
Search Engine Results Page - what users see after typing a query.
